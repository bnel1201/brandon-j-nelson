Starting my blog why and how

Hello and welcome to my first blog post! Allow me to give a little background on myself and my motivations.

About Me and why blog now

This past year has been a transformative one for me as it has for many, turning many plans upside down. I’m fortunate however to be a graduate student minimally impacted by these events.

My research in Computed Tomography (CT or CAT scan for short) and background in physics leading up till about a year ago now had me doing a lot of work on hardware, building new devices to then do research on.

So what happened a year ago? It was supposed to be 3 month downtime of my experiments to renovate our lab space. I rushed to get all the data that I thought I would need in the next 6 months to be safe before tearing down my experiment that I had labored many long days and nights on.

My daily schedule changed dramatically, from laboring in the lab space tweaking my setup and running scans every day to staring at my mess of data and MATLAB code tying it all together like a big spider web.

Long story short, I realized I needed to get organized if I was ever going to turn this mess into a paper, much less a graduate thesis. Not knowing exactly where or how to start, I turned to the internet. To my surprise I managed to stumble upon a podcast episode that managed to describe all of my problems and a solution (well a path to that solution).

On clean(er) code and automation for a grad student that knew nothing about computers

That podcast cited 3 key papersi (best read in the following order)

  • Good Enough Practices in Scientific Computing
  • A Quick Guide to Organizing Computation Biology Projects
  • Best Practices for Scientific Computing

I still have these papers bookmarked and come back to review them every fiew weeks to make to keep me on track. I hope to have another post coming out soon that dives into these works and my experiences with them in more detail for those who are interested!

As recommended in each of these papers, I started with baby steps, trying to implement one better practice at a time to get more organized and be more reproducible (Yes for other researchers to reproduce but more importantly for me to be able to reproduce my work weeks or months later!).

This was the start of my journey into programming and as I followed their guidance and started learning more I become exposed to the vibrant and supportive online community of others trying to make sense of their own work using computers and then giving back by making informative youtube videos trying to help others do the same.

Along the way I found many technical blogs such as this one on CT! that have helped me along the way.

It was finally this post that convinced me to just get started myself and see where this blog takes me.
